MSBA Capstone — Climate Risk & Health Infrastructure

  • Findings: High‑exposure clusters concentrate in remote districts with lower facility capacity and longer travel times.
  • Priority actions: strengthen critical facilities, build redundancy, and improve last‑mile access.

Objective Identify health infrastructure most exposed to floods, landslides, storms, and earthquakes across Peru, Colombia, South Africa, and Nepal to inform resilience investments.

Approach Built a composite exposure index by standardizing and weighting hazard layers with facility capacity and access indicators; used clustering/PCA to validate patterns; produced decision‑ready maps and shortlists.

Tools Python, GeoPandas, scikit‑learn (K‑Means), matplotlib/folium

Skills spatial joins, composite‑index design, clustering/PCA, map‑based storytelling for policy audiences
