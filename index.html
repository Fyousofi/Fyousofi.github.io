Spatial Risk Index for Health Infrastructure

Role: Lead Analyst · 2025 · Tools: Python, R, GIS

Problem

Decision makers needed a ranked list of health facilities exposed to floods/landslides to prioritize resilience funding.

Data

  • Health facilities: capacity, distance to PHC/hospital
  • Hazard layers: flood, landslide; admin codes (A1–A3)
  • Socio‑economic indicators: poverty, malnutrition, literacy

Methods

K‑means on standardized features; PCA for visualization; cosine similarity to composite risk profile; holdout validation.

Results

  • Top decile facilities captured a disproportionate share of modeled exposure
  • Reduced false positives vs. baseline prioritization approach
  • Generated ranked shortlist and map for operations teams

Impact

Provided an actionable pipeline for WBG teams to stage investments by district and facility tier, aligned with resilience KPIs.

Limitations

Availability of geospatial layers varies by country; sensitivity to feature scaling and administrative boundary quality.

Next steps

  • Incorporate storm surge/earthquake layers where available
  • Stability analysis across K and distance metrics
  • Scenario planning for phased investments

Airbnb Price Prediction — Washington, DC

Role: Data Analyst · 2025 · Tools: R (lm, ggplot2)

Problem

Estimate nightly prices for Airbnb listings to understand price drivers and support data‑informed pricing guidelines.

Data

  • Public Airbnb DC listings: neighborhood, room type, bedrooms, accommodates, amenities
  • Filtered outliers and missing prices; standardized categorical encodings

Methods

Compared linear regression specifications with interaction terms and log transforms; performed diagnostics (residual plots, heteroskedasticity checks) and built confidence intervals for key coefficients.

Results

  • Top price drivers: bedrooms (+$67), accommodates (+$19), and location (e.g., Shaw, Georgetown)
  • Model residuals approximately normal after log transformation; VIF checks showed acceptable multicollinearity
  • Confidence intervals quantified uncertainty for driver effects

Impact

Clarified how property and location features translate into price differences, enabling transparent pricing strategies for hosts or analysts.

Limitations

Listings data may include unobserved amenities and seasonal effects; cross‑sectional snapshot limits generalization across time.

Next steps

  • Add seasonality and event calendars; explore mixed effects by neighborhood
  • Benchmark tree‑based models to capture non‑linearities

LendingClub — Interest Rate Modeling

Role: Data Analyst · 2025 · Tools: Python, scikit‑learn, SHAP

Problem

Predict interest rates from borrower and loan features to support risk‑aware pricing.

Data

  • LendingClub dataset: loan amount, term, grade, employment length, income, purpose, etc.
  • Train/validation split with stratification over loan grade

Methods

Benchmarked regression tree, random forest, SVR, and MLP; cross‑validated using repeated K‑fold; standardized/encoded features; SHAP values for model explainability.

Results

  • Model selection based on lowest cross‑validated RMSE and stability
  • SHAP showed credit grade, income, and term as dominant drivers
  • Calibration curves flagged mild under‑prediction at higher rates

Impact

Delivered an interpretable baseline for pricing analysis and scenario testing under portfolio constraints.

Limitations

Interest rate policies vary by time; historical shifts and macro cycles may affect generalization.

Next steps

  • Hyperparameter tuning with Bayesian search; add monotonic constraints
  • Back‑testing stability across vintages; partial dependence for policy levers
